# no-invented-specifics

**Rule.** Don't invent specific weekdays, dates, or other anchored details inside narrative or takeaway beats. Use general framing — "recently", "the night before", "a few weeks ago" — unless the specific is a real fact you're reporting.

**Why.** Invented specifics lock the script to a fictional week. "She went to her GP last Tuesday" implies the listener's reality matches the avatar's. When it doesn't — when the listener didn't go on a Tuesday, isn't sitting where the script imagines, doesn't have the fictional context — the line reads as fabrication. Worse, AI-generated content is statistically over-prone to inventing specifics that read confident but aren't true. Generalising the framing keeps the script honest and removes the cadence that signals "this was generated".

**How to apply.**
- Scan finished narrative or takeaway sections for specific weekdays (Monday … Sunday) and concrete invented dates. Each hit should be either (a) a real remembered fact from the storyteller's own life, or (b) a clearly-framed example log ("Monday — breathless on the stairs"). Otherwise generalise.
- Replace "last Tuesday" → "recently"; "Sunday night" → "the night before"; "three weeks ago" → "a few weeks ago".
- Apply the same restraint to invented-specific names of places, hospitals, schools, brands. If the specific isn't load-bearing for the point, generalise it.
- The exception: when the specific *is* the point — a remembered date a survivor shares, a real product spec — keep it. Specifics are honest when they're real and dishonest when they're filler.
