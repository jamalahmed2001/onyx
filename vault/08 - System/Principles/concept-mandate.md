# concept-mandate

**Rule.** Before any production phase fires, the concept passes a single-paragraph test: relatable surface + concrete action + a hook in the first three seconds + a clear emotional or comedic engine + a principle the piece is *about*. If you can't write that paragraph, the piece isn't ready to make.

**Why.** The expensive parts of a content pipeline — synthesis, render, edit, publish — are mechanical given a strong concept and useless given a weak one. A weak concept doesn't fail loudly; it produces something polished but inert. The audience scrolls past, the writer can't pinpoint why, and the next piece repeats the mistake. The concept gate is a small phase that makes the rest of the pipeline pay off.

**How to apply.**
- The first phase of any narrative pipeline is concept-selection. Output: one paragraph satisfying the five elements.
- Relatable surface — an everyday situation the audience recognises in three seconds.
- Concrete action — something is happening, not being explained.
- Hook in the first three seconds — for short-form video especially, the audience leaves if they don't see why to stay.
- Emotional or comedic engine — what makes the audience feel something. A stakes line, a tension, a punchline.
- Principle — the underlying truth the piece is about. Not stated; demonstrated. The reason the piece exists.
- A concept that lacks any of these gets revised, not produced. Producing on a weak concept is more expensive than rewriting it.
