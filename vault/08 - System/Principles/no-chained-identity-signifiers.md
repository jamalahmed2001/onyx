# no-chained-identity-signifiers

**Rule.** Don't stack culture-specific identity tokens onto every listener. Default to inclusive low-signifier phrasing.

**Why.** A line like "tell your wife, your brother, your imam, your sister-in-law who's a nurse" assumes the listener's family structure and faith look like the narrator's. Even when the show's host belongs to a specific community and that voice is part of the show, the audience is broader than the host. Chaining culture-specific roles onto every listener excludes everyone who doesn't share that frame. Generic phrasing ("tell your partner, your brother or sister, a friend you trust") covers the same emotional ground without imposing a template.

**How to apply.**
- The host's faith, region, or community register is part of the show's voice — keep it in the host's own lines (greeting, dua, idiom).
- The listener-facing lines stay inclusive: "your partner" not "your wife"; "a friend you trust" not "your imam"; "someone in your family" not stacked specific roles.
- Scan finished scripts for ladders of identity-specific roles. Replace ladders with one or two low-signifier phrases.
- Exception: when the episode is genuinely *about* a specific community (a piece on faith leadership, on extended-family caregiving, on a culturally specific topic), the specifics belong — don't sanitise the actual subject.
